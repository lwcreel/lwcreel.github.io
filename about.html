I know, I know, horizontal stripes aren't my best look. In my defense, I was only a freshman when this photo was taken and, in my experience, freshmen have a lot to learn about a lot of things, not just fashion. As you can probably tell, this photo was taken around Christmas time of one of the most eventful years of my life. I had only recently made the decision to major in Computer Science at Texas A&M University, a decision which I firmly believe was the best decision of my life. In this photo you can see my older brother and sister, as well as my parents and the puppy, Sealy. The house we are in front of is my grandmother's, which I, along with my brother and the rest of our family, helped my father to build. There were always projects around the farm that needed to be done growing up, and helping and working alongside my dad to complete these jobs helped me develop a love for doing and a work ethic that I carry to this day.

My love for programming stems from the love of doing that I developed growing up. There's something incredibly satisfying about building something from scratch that, before you, did not exist. This website is a perfect example. I would be lying if I said that I considered making this site, or any of the other projects, work because I have so much fun doing, making things that did not exist before.

I was first introduced to computers as a medium for creation in a high school class that, at the time, I thought was completely useless. The story is actually interesting enough to relate here. So, I went to a small school. Like, really small. Thirty people in my whole grade small. One of the requirements to graduate high school in Texas is two consecutive years of a foreign language. Being that Spanish is the second most spoken language here, it shouldn't come as a surprise that almost everybody fills this requirement with that language. My freshman year, like every other student in my grade, I took Spanish I and passed easily (since my mom's family is Puerto Rican, I had a lot of prior exposure to the language despite not being a speaker of it myself). The trouble came when our Spanish teacher unexpectedly left the school the Summer before my sophomore year.

Being a very small school, it's difficult enough to attract teachers already, and my school was unable to hire a Spanish teacher in time for the new school year. Naturally, my school decided that my grade would take Computer Science classes to count as our foreign language. To this day, I don't know how they swung that, but I'm glad they did. I had already taken Computer Science I, which at the time was a class on HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and had a blast doing it. This sparked my love for computers, but Computer Science II was created just for my grade and it showed me a whole new world. Computer Science II was essentially a self-taught course, they gave us a book on Java, some computers, and a test every six weeks. In this class I realized that computers could make complicated tasks easy (and simple tasks hard).

Computer Science II sparked a love for programming that turned into a flame when I was exposed to MATLAB my freshman year at Texas A&M. Though I initially wanted to do Aerospace Engineering, I switched to Computer Science when I realized that the programming I did in MATLAB (which everyone on my team invariably hated) was what I enjoyed the most out of all my coursework. I switched to Computer Science and never looked back.

Other than my coursework, there are two organizations on-campus at Texas A&M University that have played a significant role in my life: Fish Camp and MSC ALOT. A bit of background for those uninitiated to any of TAMU's sub-cults, Fish Camp is a four day extended freshman orientation held at Lakeview Methodist Conference Center and MSC ALOT is the Memorial Student Center's Aggie Leaders of Tomorrow, a Freshman Leadership Organization that puts on programs run by freshmen. I have been a Fish Camp counselor for the past two summers, and I have learned so much about myself in the process. Counselors are responsible for leading Discussion Groups (DG) and for helping freshmen to adjust to Texas A&M. I decided to become a counelor after my experience freshman year. Coming from such a small school, A&M was a huge culture shock for me. Luckily I had the support of my DG "parents" to help me orient myself in the new environment. Because of this experience, I resolved to help support the incoming freshmen in the same way that I had been supported.

The experiences I have had as part of MSC ALOT are irreplaceable in my life. My freshman year, I was a part of the subcommittee responsible for organizing and running TEDxTAMU, A&M's annual TED talk. My sophomore year, I was an assistant director in the subcommittee that organized Angel Tree, a Salvation Army program to bring Christmas to underpriviliged children. I am happy to have helped the program raise a record number of toys (over 1100) for Angel Tree. Through both of these programs I learned much about managing large scale projects and time-management against deadlines.

Well, that's really all I care to tell some strangers on the internet about myself. Hopefully you enjoyed reading a bit about who I am. And if not, well that doesn't matter all that much to me. I had a great time making this page and writing all this down, and you made it to the end regardless, so as we say in Aggieland, thanks and gig 'em!
